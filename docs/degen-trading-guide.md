# Degen Trading Guide: How To Trade EVOs Without Getting Rekt

> Listen up. This is for the degens. The ones who ape first and read docs never. This is your survival guide.

---

## The TL;DR

EVOs are NFTs with SOL locked inside. You can always get the SOL back by shattering (destroying the EVO). The locked SOL is your floor. You can't go below it (minus a small fee). Everything above the floor is pure degen premium.

This is the first asset on Solana where you actually have downside protection built into the chain. Not a promise. Not a "community floor." Actual SOL you can reclaim.

---

## The Basics (Read This If You Read Nothing Else)

### What You Are Buying

An EVO is a Solana account with SOL inside it. You own the account. The SOL is yours. You can:
- **Shatter it** and take the SOL back (minus a fee)
- **Feed it** more SOL to raise the floor
- **Trade it** on the built-in marketplace
- **Evolve it** if the collection supports it (visual stages)
- **Hold it** and hope the premium goes up

### The Floor

```
floor = locked SOL minus shatter fee
```

If an EVO has 1 SOL locked and a 10% shatter fee, your floor is 0.9 SOL. You can shatter anytime and get 0.9 SOL. No lockup. No permission. No waiting.

This means you NEVER have to sell below 0.9 SOL. If someone lists below floor, you buy and shatter. Free money.

### The Premium

Everything above the floor is the premium. The premium is what makes it degen. It can go to zero. It can 10x. It depends on:
- How hot the collection is
- How old the EVO is (old = rare)
- How many times it's been traded (provenance)
- How much SOL is locked (big = flex)
- How many have been shattered (less supply = more scarce)

## Strategies For Degens

### 1. The Floor Sweep (Low Risk, Low Reward)

Find EVOs listed below or near floor. Buy them. Shatter them. Pocket the difference.

Example: EVO has 2 SOL locked, 5% shatter fee (0.1 SOL). Floor is 1.9 SOL. Someone lists it for 1.5 SOL because they panic sold. You buy for 1.5 SOL, shatter, get 1.9 SOL. Profit: 0.4 SOL. Risk: almost zero (the floor is guaranteed).

This is boring but it works. In thin markets or new collections, people misprice. Be patient. Watch the listings.

### 2. The Early Mint (Medium Risk, High Reward)

Mint EVOs from new collections early. If the collection takes off, you are holding early mints with good provenance. The premium can be significant.

Risk: the collection might flop. But your downside is the floor. If you minted with 0.5 SOL locked, you can always shatter and get most of it back. You are not holding a bag to zero like with regular NFTs.

Key: evaluate the creator, the art, the supply cap, and the lock amount before minting. A 100-cap collection with 0.5 SOL lock from a known creator is a better bet than a 10,000-cap collection with 0.01 SOL lock from a random wallet.

### 3. The Feed Flex (High Cost, Status Play)

Buy an EVO. Feed it a lot of SOL. The floor goes up. The art gets bigger. You now own the most fed EVO in the collection. That is a flex. People pay for flexes.

Risk: you are locking more SOL. But it is still your SOL (minus shatter fee). You are not spending it, you are storing it in a flex asset. If you need the SOL back, shatter.

This works best in collections with small supply and active communities. Feeding a dead collection does nothing.

### 4. The Provenance Play (Long Term, Patience Required)

Buy EVOs and trade them. A lot. Every trade adds a fracture line to the art. An EVO that has been traded 50 times has 50 fracture lines. It has a story. Collectors pay for stories.

This is the long game. You are building provenance over weeks or months. The EVO becomes more visually distinct and more historically significant. At some point, someone wants THAT EVO because of its history, not just its floor.

### 5. The Shatter Arbitrage (Active Trading)

Watch for collections where people are shattering a lot. Supply is burning. The survivors are getting more scarce. Buy survivors before the supply burn makes them rare.

Example: a collection started with 1,000 EVOs. 800 have been shattered. 200 survive. Those 200 are now 5x more scarce than at launch. If the collection has any demand, the survivors should trade up.

### 6. The Evolution Flip (RevealAndEvolve Collections)

In collections that evolve, EVOs at later stages might be rarer (if most people haven't evolved yet) or more desirable (if the evolved art is better). Buy unevolved, evolve, sell evolved. Or buy evolved if they're being undervalued.

Understand the evolution conditions. Some require feeding (costs SOL), some require trading (costs time), some require holding (costs patience). Factor these into your profit calc.

## Red Flags (Do Not Buy If)

### The lock amount is 0.01 SOL

The floor is basically zero. This is just a regular NFT with extra steps. No downside protection. Skip it or treat it as a pure degen play.

### The shatter fee is 20%

You lose 20% of your SOL if you shatter. That is a huge cut. The floor is real but the fee eats it. Only buy if you are very confident in the premium.

### The supply cap is 20,000 and only 50 have minted

19,950 more can be minted at any time. That is massive inflation risk. The creator can dump more supply whenever. Stick to collections that are closer to sold out.

### The creator is a brand new wallet

No history, no reputation, no track record. The floor protects your SOL but the premium can go to zero instantly. If you would not buy a regular NFT from this creator, do not buy an EVO from them either.

### The royalty is 25%

Every time you sell, you lose 25% of the sale price to the creator. That is brutal for active trading. You need the price to go up 25%+ just to break even on a round trip. Stick to lower royalty collections for trading.

## Green Flags (Good Signs)

### Lock amount is 0.1 to 1 SOL

Real floor. Real protection. Not too expensive to mint. This is the sweet spot.

### Shatter fee is 5 to 10%

Enough to discourage panic shattering (preserves supply) but not so high that it traps you. The floor is still meaningful.

### Supply cap is 100 to 1,000

Scarcity is built in. Even if the collection is not famous, the limited supply supports the premium.

### Creator has a track record

Previous collections, known wallet, community presence. The premium is more likely to hold.

### Collection is burning supply

People are shattering. Supply is going down. Survivors are getting rare. This is bullish.

## The Math You Need To Do

### Before Minting

```
total cost = mint price + lock amount
floor = lock amount * (1 - shatter fee / 100)
worst case loss = lock amount * (shatter fee / 100)
```

If mint price is 0.1 SOL, lock is 0.5 SOL, shatter fee is 10%:
- Total cost: 0.6 SOL
- Floor: 0.45 SOL
- Worst case: you lose 0.05 SOL (the shatter fee on 0.5 SOL)

You are risking 0.05 SOL to potentially gain the premium. That is the degen math.

### Before Selling

```
you receive = sale price * (1 - royalty / 100)
profit = you receive - what you paid
```

If you bought at 1 SOL, sell at 2 SOL, royalty is 10%:
- You receive: 1.8 SOL
- Profit: 0.8 SOL

If you bought at 1 SOL, sell at 1 SOL, royalty is 10%:
- You receive: 0.9 SOL
- Loss: 0.1 SOL

Factor the royalty into every trade. It is not optional.

```
you receive = locked SOL * (1 - shatter fee / 100)
loss vs what you paid = what you paid - you receive
```

- You receive: 0.9 SOL (1 SOL minus 10%)
- Loss: 0.2 SOL (the mint price + the shatter fee)

The mint price is gone (it went to the creator). The shatter fee is gone (it went to the fee destination). You get back the locked SOL minus fee. That is your floor.

## Common Ways To Get Rekt

### 1. Buying Above Floor And Watching Premium Go To Zero

You paid 5 SOL for an EVO with 1 SOL locked. The premium was 4 SOL. The collection dies. Premium goes to zero. You shatter and get 0.9 SOL (1 SOL minus 10% fee). You lost 4.1 SOL.

The floor protects the locked SOL. It does not protect the premium you paid. Know this before you buy.

### 2. Forgetting The Royalty On Sale

You bought at 2 SOL. The collection pumps. You list at 4 SOL. It sells. You get 3.6 SOL (10% royalty). You made 1.6 SOL, not 2 SOL. The royalty is real and it is taken automatically.

### 3. Feeding A Dead Collection

You feed 5 SOL into your EVO. The floor is now 6 SOL. But nobody wants to buy the EVO because the collection is dead. You can shatter and get 5.4 SOL back (6 SOL minus 10% fee). You did not lose much, but you locked 5 SOL for nothing.

Feeding only makes sense if the collection has active demand. Otherwise you are just locking SOL in a PDA for no reason.

### 4. Not Checking The Supply Cap

You buy into a 10,000-cap collection at mint 50. The creator keeps minting. Supply goes to 5,000. Your EVO is now one of 5,000, not one of 50. The premium collapses.

Check the cap vs the minted count. If there is a lot of headroom, the supply can dilute you.

### 5. Panic Shattering

The market dips. You panic. You shatter your EVO and take the 10% fee hit. Two days later the collection pumps. You would have been fine if you held.

The floor is there for your protection. It is not there for you to panic exit at the worst time. Use it when you actually need the SOL, not when you are emotional.

EVOs give you something regular NFTs do not: a floor. Your locked SOL is always there. You can always get it back. This changes the game.

But the floor only protects the locked SOL. The premium is still degen. It can go up 10x or go to zero. The floor is your safety net, not your profit guarantee.

Do the math. Check the parameters. Understand the collection. Then ape.
